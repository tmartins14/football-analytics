# Phase 1: The Vocabulary Foundation
**Goal:** Build tactical vocabulary sufficient to identify a defensible 
analytical problem worth investigating

---

## What This Phase Is

This phase is not about becoming a tactical expert. It is about building 
enough vocabulary to watch football analytically, read practitioner 
literature critically, and identify problems that real analysts care about. 
The hidden agenda is finding your football problem — a specific, defensible 
analytical question you can pursue in later phases.

Every session has one job: react, don't summarize.

## Weekly Writing Exercise

One paragraph per week. A reaction, not a summary.
Prompt: **What felt incomplete, wrong, or unconvincing in what I just 
consumed?**

This is not optional. The paragraph is where thinking actually develops.

## Key Vocabulary Built This Phase
- Pressing triggers
- Cover shadows
- Gegenpressing / counter-pressing
- High block / mid block / low block
- PPDA (passes per defensive action)
- Defensive line height
- Press resistance
- Pressing traps
- Man-oriented vs space-oriented defense
- Resource constraint vs philosophical low block

---

## What Success Looks Like

By the end of this phase you should be able to:
- Watch a match and identify which defensive shape a team is using and why
- Read a Spielverlagerung article without losing the thread
- Articulate at least one candidate analytical problem in one defensible 
sentence
- Explain why that problem would matter to a club analyst
