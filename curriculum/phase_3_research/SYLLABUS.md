# Phase 3: Self-Directed Research
**Duration:** Open-ended | **Time commitment:** 1 hour/day minimum
**Prerequisite:** Phase 2 complete, research question selected and 
preliminarily validated with data

**Goal:** Produce one piece of original, defensible analytical work on a 
football problem that practitioners would find credible

---

## What This Phase Is

There is no prescribed curriculum here. Phase 3 is structured around your 
specific research question, your data access, and your methodology. What is 
prescribed is the standard of output and the process discipline required to 
get there.

This phase ends when you have produced something you would be comfortable 
defending in a room full of people who know more about football than you do.

---

## Phase 3 Has Four Stages

### Stage 1: Problem Definition
**Output:** `research-question.md`

This document must answer:
- What is the specific question you are investigating?
- Why would a club analyst care about this?
- What has existing literature said about it?
- What gap are you filling?
- What would a definitive answer look like?

Do not proceed to Stage 2 until this document is tight enough that someone 
could read it and immediately understand what you are doing and why it 
matters.

---

### Stage 2: Methodology
**Output:** `methodology.md`

This document must answer:
- What data are you using and why?
- What are the limitations of that data?
- What analytical method are you using?
- What assumptions does that method make?
- How will you know if your hypothesis is wrong?

The last question is the most important. If you cannot articulate how your 
hypothesis could be falsified, you do not have a research question — you 
have a confirmation exercise.

---

### Stage 3: Analysis
**Output:** Documented notebooks in `work/notebooks/`

Standards:
- Every notebook must be reproducible — someone else should be able to 
clone the repo and run it
- Every analytical choice must be documented with a rationale
- Negative results must be documented, not hidden
- Visualizations must be publication quality — use mplsoccer standards

---

### Stage 4: Output
**Output:** A written piece suitable for publication

This is not a blog post summarizing what you did. It is an analytical 
argument with:
- A clear claim
- Evidence that supports it
- An honest account of limitations
- Implications for practitioners

**Target outlets to consider:**
- StatsBomb conference submissions
- Sloan Sports Analytics Conference
- The Athletic / Tifo (longer term)
- Your own Substack as a portfolio piece

---

## Standards That Apply Throughout

**On rigor:**
A finding that holds up under scrutiny is worth more than ten interesting 
observations that don't. If your result depends on a specific subset of 
data or a particular methodological choice, say so explicitly.

**On honesty:**
If the data kills your hypothesis, document it. A well-reasoned null result 
is more impressive to practitioners than a forced positive finding. It shows 
you know how to think.

**On scope:**
Do one thing well. The most common failure mode in independent research is 
trying to answer three questions at once and answering none of them 
properly.

---

## What Success Looks Like

By the end of this phase you have:
- One piece of original analytical work you can defend publicly
- A documented methodology that someone else could reproduce
- A GitHub repository that demonstrates serious, sustained analytical 
thinking over an extended period
- Something concrete to discuss in a job interview at a football club

---

## A Note on Timeline

This phase has no fixed end date by design. A rushed piece of research that 
doesn't hold up is worse than no research at all. Take the time it takes. 
The discipline is in the daily hour — not in hitting an arbitrary deadline.
